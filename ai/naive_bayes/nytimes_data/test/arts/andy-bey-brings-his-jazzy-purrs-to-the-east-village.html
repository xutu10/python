http://www.nytimes.com/2014/09/14/arts/music/andy-bey-brings-his-jazzy-purrs-to-the-east-village.html 2014-09-15 23:27:36 Andy Bey Brings His Jazzy Purrs to the East Village The dark, slow and trenchant sound of Andy Bey will be at Cafe Noctambulo, a new club at Pangea in the East Village. === The bass-baritone voice of the jazz singer Andy Bey is elemental and complicated, a purr and a pushed-out moan, a compound of tones from different parts of his body. It was created through circumstance: working in strict-discipline harmony and phrasing with his sisters in the early 1960s, making his words punch over the sound of Horace Silver’s group in the 1970s and isolated dedication thereafter to a solo voice-and-piano concept. His dark, slow and trenchant sound can feel almost supernatural, even when he’s singing the most familiar repertory in American music. He’s best heard on his own, as he will be at Cafe Noctambulo, a new club at Pangea in the East Village. (8 p.m. and 10 p.m. Friday and Saturday, 178 Second Avenue, between 11th and 12th Streets; 212-995-0900,
