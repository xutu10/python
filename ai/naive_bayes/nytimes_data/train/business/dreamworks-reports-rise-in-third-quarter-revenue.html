Revenue totaled $180.9 million. The results were better than most analysts had predicted, sending the studio’s shares up as much as 7 percent in after-hours trading Although DreamWorks Animation has been racing to build meaningful TV and consumer products businesses, the company still has only one division that matters: feature film. The successful third quarter, which came after two consecutive quarters of losses, turned on the release of a single film, “How to Train Your Dragon 2.” Distributed by 20th Century Fox, “How to Train Your Dragon 2” had a soft performance in the United States but strong interest overseas, ultimately delivering more than $615 million at the worldwide box office. Next up is “The Penguins of Madagascar” on Nov. 26. “Penguins” cost $132 million to make. During the conference call, analysts asked Mr. Katzenberg about the schedule ahead, when the studio plans to release a string of four original films. DreamWorks Animation has lately struggled to find new hits, leaning on franchises like “Dragon” and “Madagascar” for results. Mr. Katzenberg left the door open to scheduling changes, but downplayed concerns. “I’m not as nervous about the original titles as you are,” he said. In particular, Mr. Katzenberg noted that one release, “Trolls,” a musical comedy based on the wild-haired toy line, will come with a substantial consumer products push.
